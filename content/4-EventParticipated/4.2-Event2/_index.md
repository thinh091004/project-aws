# Report on the “GenAI-powered App-DB Modernization Workshop”

### Event Objectives

- Career Orientation: To help 3rd and 4th year students prepare the necessary mindset and thinking skills (critical thinking, problem-solving, data storytelling) in the face of changing job markets.

- Sharing Practical Work Experiences: To decode the detailed roles of positions such as Data Analytics Engineer and DevOps Engineer in businesses, helping participants understand "what they actually do" instead of relying solely on theoretical job descriptions (JDs).

- Decoding Corporate Culture: To share insights into the operations and work culture of multinational corporations (MNCs), including standard recruitment processes and core values ​​such as the "no-blame" culture.

- Introduction to the development roadmap and community: Introducing programs such as the AWS Student Builder Group, AWS Community Builder, and the roadmap from student to AWS Partner.

- Supplementary skills and tools: Guidance on foundational skills (Linux, Networking, CI/CD) and how to leverage AI to optimize work performance instead of relying entirely on it.

### Featured Content

#### Career Orientation and Development Mindset
* Competency Development Model: Speaker Dat Pham shares a development roadmap from Follower -> Learner -> Problem Solver -> System Thinker -> Super Star.

* Core Mindset for Students: Emphasizing 4 essential skills: Critical Thinking, Communication Skills, Data Storytelling, and Problem Solving.

* No-Blame Culture: A key aspect of the culture at multinational technology corporations, where mistakes are used to analyze root causes and improve systems instead of criticizing individuals.

#### Job Practices: Data Analytics & DevOps

#### Data Analytics Engineer (DAE):
* At startups (like Kamereo): Focus on building dashboards, monitoring operational performance, and identifying the causes of fluctuations in business metrics (such as GMV).

* At large corporations (like Colgate-Palmolive): Participate in IoT projects, optimize production costs, and implement digital transformation in factories.

#### DevOps Engineer:
* Job Practice: It's not just about writing CI/CD or using Docker/K8s, but about understanding how applications operate, automating repetitive tasks, and ensuring system stability.

* Advice: "Tools may change, but the platform doesn't." Mastering Linux, networking, Git, and systems thinking is essential.

* Using AI: It's recommended to use AI to leverage skills, not to "shut down" your work.

#### Community Ecosystem and Networking Opportunities
* AWS Program: Introducing pathways from the AWS Student Builder Group for students to the AWS Community Builder and becoming a professional AWS partner.

* Real-world Project: Sharing how to build a scalable URL shortening service on the AWS platform.

#### Recruitment Process at Multinational Corporations (MNCs)

* Revealing the standard 4-step process: Application Screening (ATS) -> Skills Test (Logic/Technical) -> Professional Interview (STAR ​​model) -> Cultural Fit Assessment with Leadership.

### What I Learned

#### Career Mindset and Personal Development
* The 5-Level Competency Model: Don't overemphasize job titles; instead, focus on self-improvement through the stages: Follower, Learner, Problem Solver, System Thinker, and Super Star.

* "Golden" Skills for Students: To adapt to changing job markets, you need to equip yourself with:

* Critical Thinking: The ability to objectively analyze data to make informed judgments.

* Communication Skills: Clearly conveying ideas.

* Data Storytelling: Transforming dry data into meaningful information and driving action.

* Problem Solving: Finding optimal solutions based on data.

#### Practical Lessons in Data and DevOps

* Data Analytics Engineer: The reality of the job is very diverse depending on the size of the enterprise. In a startup, you need to focus on dashboards and identify the causes of metric fluctuations (like GMV), while in a large corporation like FMCG, you might participate in IoT projects and factory operational optimization.

* DevOps Engineer:
* Platform is more important than tools: Tools are always changing, but fundamental knowledge like Linux, Networking, Git, and systems thinking is constant.

* Understand "Why" before knowing "How": Don't just copy commands without understanding the underlying principles.

* Automation mindset: Always look for ways to automate repetitive tasks.
#### Work Culture in Multinational Corporations (MNCs)
* No-Blame Culture: When system errors occur, engineers focus on finding the root cause to fix the system instead of criticizing individuals.

* Caring & Inclusive Culture: Putting people at the center, respecting diversity, and continuously improving.

* Global Standards: Learning from models like Wakon Yosai (Japan) or the Miracle on the Han River (Korea) about maintaining identity while mastering advanced technology and adhering to stringent international standards.

#### Tools and AI
* Leveraging Intelligent AI: Using AI as a lever to enhance skills, absolutely not letting AI "shut down" your brain during work.

* Practical Experience: Building small projects, self-deploying, self-breaking, and self-fixing is the best way to learn (Deploy, Break, Fix).

### Application to Work

#### Upgrading Thinking and Work Skills (Soft Skills)
* Apply the STAR model in communication and interviews: When reporting results or interviewing at large corporations (MNCs), present using the following structure: Situation - Task - Action - Result.

* Practice Data Storytelling: Instead of just presenting dry numbers, transform them into meaningful information to drive action (for example, not just reporting a sales decline, but identifying the reasons for GMV fluctuations and proposing solutions).

* Develop Systems Thinking: Always look at the big picture, understand the connections between departments, predict operational risks, and assess long-term financial impacts instead of focusing on fixing minor errors.

#### Applications in Engineering and Operations (Technical Skills)
* No-Blame Operational Culture: When system failures occur, focus on root cause analysis to improve the system, rather than assigning individual blame.

* Prioritize Platform Over Tools: Whether working in DevOps or AI, focus on mastering core knowledge such as Linux, Networking, Git, and how applications actually operate. Tools may change, but the platform will help you adapt quickly.

* Automate Repetitive Tasks: Always look for ways to automate boring and repeatable tasks to optimize performance and minimize errors.

* Use AI as a Lever: Leverage AI to enhance skills and work efficiency, but absolutely do not let AI stop you from thinking independently.

#### Personal Career Development Pathway
* Identify your current skill level: Assess your current level (Follower, Learner, Problem Solver, System Thinker, or Super Star) to create a suitable learning plan, instead of just chasing titles.

* Build a real-world project (Build - Break - Fix): The best way to learn is to build a small project (like a URL shortening service on the Cloud), deploy it, make mistakes, and find ways to fix them.

* Connect with the expert community: Join networks like LinkedIn and community groups (AWS Student Builder Group, AWS Community Builder) to update your knowledge and expand your career opportunities.
